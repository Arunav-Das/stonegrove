Stonegrove — Recommendation Engine: Silent Strugglers / Thrivers / Rest

This report covers the totality of students currently in the knowledge graph -- every student is classified into exactly one category, peer mentors are recommended for silent strugglers from the silent-thriver pool, and concrete interventions are suggested per flagged student. Journey similarity is computed from each student's OWN ordinal stage sequence (Year1, Year2, ...), not the calendar academic_year label, so students who started in different calendar cohorts (e.g. 1048-49 vs 1050-51) remain comparable at the same point in their journey.

Student coverage. 35,000 of an expected 35,000 students (100.0%) are present as sg:Student nodes in the source graph.

1. All students, by detection category

Every loaded student falls into exactly one bucket: SILENT_STRUGGLER (struggling + quiet), VISIBLE (struggling or thriving but already visibly engaged -- a check-in would likely catch them already), SILENT_THRIVER (thriving + quiet -- candidate peer mentors), or NEUTRAL_REST (no strong signal, explicitly left alone).

2. Journey-shape embedding space

Each point is a student, positioned by the stage-aware structural shape of their academic journey. Department, outcome, and demographic fields are excluded from this embedding by design.

3. ⚠ Fairness diagnostic

Share of each group classified SILENT_STRUGGLER should track that group's actual struggle rate. A gap of 15 percentage points or more is flagged for review -- it may indicate the detection flag is picking up something correlated with group identity rather than genuine risk.
